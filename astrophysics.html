Learn more about Astrophysics!

Cosmology

Cosmology is the study of the universe as a whole, including its origins, evolution, structure, and eventual fate. It encompasses the Big Bang theory, the cosmic microwave background, dark matter, and dark energy. Cosmologists aim to understand the large-scale properties of the universe and the laws that govern its behavior.

Stellar Astrophysics

Stellar astrophysics focuses on the study of stars, including their formation, evolution, and eventual demise. It examines the physical properties of stars, their internal and external processes, and their interactions with surrounding environments. Stellar astrophysics is crucial for understanding the life cycles of stars and their impact on the cosmos.

Galactic Astrophysics

Galactic astrophysics studies the structure, dynamics, and interactions of galaxies. It includes the examination of the Milky Way and other galaxies, their formation, and their evolution over time. Researchers in this field seek to understand the processes that shape galaxies and the role they play in the broader universe.

High-Energy Astrophysics

High-energy astrophysics investigates the most energetic phenomena in the universe, such as supernovae, gamma-ray bursts, and black holes. This subdivision explores the processes that produce high-energy radiation and particles, providing insights into extreme environments and fundamental physical laws.

Planetary Astrophysics

Planetary astrophysics focuses on the study of planets, both within our solar system and in other star systems (exoplanets). It examines planetary formation, composition, atmospheres, and potential for habitability. This field is essential for understanding the diversity of planetary systems and the potential for life beyond Earth.

Astroparticle Physics

Astroparticle physics bridges the gap between astrophysics and particle physics. It studies cosmic rays, neutrinos, and other subatomic particles originating from space. This subdivision aims to uncover the fundamental particles and forces that govern the universe, providing insights into both cosmology and particle physics.
